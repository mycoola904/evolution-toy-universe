# Fundamental Laws of the Toy Universe

**Status:** Future design consideration

As the core simulation documents were developed, several fundamental laws naturally emerged. These are not implementation details, but the underlying "physics" that govern the Toy Universe. They may eventually deserve their own section in `design.md`.

## 1. Conservation of Energy

Energy is the universal currency of the simulation.

- Energy enters the universe only through environmental generation.
- Energy moves between the environment and organisms through deterministic transfers.
- Energy is consumed through metabolism and organism actions.
- Energy may be recycled when organisms die.
- No organism or subsystem may create energy from nothing.

---

## 2. Uniform Time

Time advances uniformly for the entire universe.

- The simulation proceeds in discrete ticks.
- Every organism experiences every tick.
- Every organism pays basal metabolism every tick.
- No organism may skip, accelerate, or rewind time.

---

## 3. Universal Physical Laws

Nothing is special.

Every entity in the universe obeys the same rules.

- Organisms are not privileged.
- Energy follows identical accounting everywhere.
- The environment obeys deterministic rules.
- Evolution arises from interactions with these shared laws rather than exceptions.

---

## Observation

These laws were not designed first—they emerged naturally while simplifying the simulation.

This is encouraging, as it suggests the Toy Universe is developing a coherent internal "physics" rather than a collection of unrelated mechanics.

Future versions of the documentation may formalize these as the foundational laws from which all other models are derived.
